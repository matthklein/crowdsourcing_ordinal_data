Assume we are dealing with three objects, say object A, object B and object C. Assume we have some notion of assessing similarity between these objects, that is we could assign some numbers quantifying how similar two of the three objects are to each other.

Let us denote these numbers by similarity(A,B), similarity(A,C) and similarity(B,C). A high value of similarity(A,B) means that objects A and B are very similar to each other whereas a low value means that they are quite dissimilar. The numbers similarity(A,C) and similarity(B,C) have a similar meaning.

We say that object A is the most representative/most central object within the objects A, B and C if it holds that

similarity(A,B) > similarity(B,C)     AND     similarity(A,C) > similarity(B,C).


Consider the following example:

In the following three images you are shown sneakers, rubber boots and slippers.

All of them are shoes. Clearly, the rubber boots and the slippers are very dissimilar, but the sneakers are not so dissimilar from either the rubber boots or the slippers. Hence similarity(sneakers,rubber boots) > similarity(rubber boots,slippers) and similarity(sneakers,slippers) > similarity(rubber boots,slippers). The sneakers are the most central object!


In the survey you should determine the most central object in a triplet of cars. Of course, in most cases (perhaps in all?) there is no true answer. Your answer depends on how you perceive and assess similarity between cars!
